Scope and Mission

The IEEE CIS Task Force on Physics-Informed Evolutionary Learning and Optimization (PiELaO) is dedicated to advancing computational intelligence in the domain of scientific machine learning by embedding the knowledge of physical systems directly into neural network architectures and evolutionary optimization operators. By integrating mathematical modelling and physics-guided evolutionary computation, PiELaO aims to develop learning algorithms capable of modelling multi-scale, high-dimensional, and non-linear dynamical systems, overcoming challenges of scalability and computational intensity that traditional methods often face. This mission aligns closely with the CIS’s focus on intelligent systems, adaptive learning, and data-driven optimization. PiELaO promotes the design and application of learning algorithms that solve forward, inverse, and control problems across various fields where physical laws are critical. The Task Force supports the development of scalable, real-time frameworks that integrate evolutionary principles, machine learning and fundamental physical laws, pushing the boundaries of computational intelligence in practical and theoretical applications.

The IEEE Task Force on PiELaO aims to enhance the cutting-edge computational intelligence methods by leveraging the strengths of data-driven AI approaches and physical models, and bring together academic researchers and business practitioners to achieve both theoretical breakthroughs and industrial innovation. The key objectives are to: (a) advance the PiELaO research and innovation (topics as listed below) through joint effort from researchers in the focused regions of Asia and UK, and (b) build up the academic and enterprise engagement across the two regions.

  1. Mathematical Foundations for Intelligent Physics Integration:
    • Strengthen the theoretical foundations of Physics-Informed learning systems, such as (but not limited to) physics-informed neural networks (PINNs), through multi-scale operators and nonlinear dynamical systems.
    • Investigate methods to enhance model flexibility for irregular domains and dynamic boundary conditions
    • Explore the stability and convergence properties of PINNs by examining function space topologies and leveraging neuro-symbolic computing.
  2. Evolutionary Optimization and Computational Techniques for Intelligent Systems:
    • Develop evolutionary algorithms and metaheuristic optimization techniques suitable for high-dimensional, non-linear parameter spaces found in multi-physics systems.
    • Balancing data-driven and physics-informed loss terms through evolutionary multi-objective optimization frameworks.
    • Employ data-driven evolutionary algorithms for inverse problem-solving that measure discrepancies within physics-based constraints. This objective aligns with the CIS emphasis on neural networks and adaptive systems to infer critical model parameters.
    • Study evolutionary algorithms for physics-informed optimal experimental design for inverse problems.
    • Expand the scope of PINNs to handle stochastic systems by incorporating probabilistic modeling, Bayesian inference, and random field approximations.
  3. Scalable Computational Intelligence Frameworks:
    • Focus on computational methods that enable physics-informed learning to scale efficiently on high-performance computing (HPC) systems using GPU acceleration, parallel processing, and distributed architectures. These approaches will enable effective solutions in complex, heterogeneous domains, supporting real-time modelling and dynamic analysis.
    • Research novel evolution strategies for adaptive selection of collocation points to minimize computational overhead, allowing for efficient, high-fidelity modelling.
    • Advance evolutionary meta-learning, transfer evolutionary optimization and evolutionary multitasking as complementary approaches to achieve generalizable physic-informed learning architectures and algorithms.
  4. Applications in Multi-Physics and Interdisciplinary Challenges:
    • Address multi-physics problems governed by coupled PDEs across multiple physical domains. This includes scenarios involving fluid-structure interactions, electromagnetic fields, heat transfer, and quantum systems, where PINNs can enforce interface compatibility and accommodate hybrid phenomena.
    • Enhance model robustness in fields with sparse, noisy, or high-cost data, such as biomedical engineering, geophysical monitoring, and environmental science.
    • Develop frameworks that integrate fuzzy logic, explainable AI, and symbolic reasoning within PINNs, connecting learned representations with physical quantities of interest, aligning with our focus on transparent and accountable AI systems.
  5. Educational Outreach and Industry Engagement:
    • Organize workshops, conferences, and seminars to facilitate knowledge-sharing on computational intelligence and physics-informed learning. The Task Force aims to build an interdisciplinary community by engaging researchers and practitioners in applied mathematics, engineering, and data science.
    • Partner with industry to translate research into practical applications, developing open-source tools and software libraries for physics-informed scientific computing. By making these resources accessible, PiELaO encourages widespread adoption and fosters an ecosystem of collaborative innovation within the broader computational intelligence community.
    • Support initiatives for open-source development in physics-informed computational intelligence, enhancing accessibility and reproducibility in multi-physics modeling and data-driven optimization. The Task Force will advocate for standards and best practices that align with transparent AI principles.

Planned Regional Activities

The primary goal of the PiELaO task force is to promote the newly advanced physics-informed evolutionary learning and optimization research and build a linkage between the PiELaO researchers and practitioners to achieve research and technological innovation across the Asian and British Regions. We have successfully organized several research activities (below) in the past years and identified the PiELaO critical mass in the evolutionary computation community.

  • Special Session on Physics-informed Computational Intelligence: Theories, Models and Applications in the 2022 IEEE Symposium Series on Computational Intelligence
  • Special Session on Physics-Informed Computational Intelligence: Algorithms and Applications in the 2024 IEEE World Congress on Computational Intelligence
  • Workshop on Physics-Informed Evolutionary Learning and Optimization in the 2025 IEEE Congress on Evolutionary Computation

Accordingly, we are planning a series of academic workshops, journal special issues and academic and enterprise engagement events over the next two years.

Year 1: We are organizing one special issue on Physics-Informed Evolutionary Computation: Advances and Applications in IEEE Transactions on Evolutionary Computation in 2025 (the proposal has been accepted). In additional, we will collaborate with industrial partners, national/regional funding agency and research institutes to organize academic and enterprise engagement events and grow a wider PiELaO community. One potential event is to be organized in the Summer/Winter of 2025.

Year 2: We will submit one special issue to IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, and organize one workshop in either IEEE World Congress on Computational Intelligence, IEEE Symposium Series on Computational Intelligence, or IEEE CIS-sponsored ones. Another academic and enterprise engagement event will be organized in 2026.

We will organize a regular online technical seminar joined by invited academic/industrial speakers on PiELaO and plan the activities in a quarterly task force committee meeting.

In addition, we aim to provide sustained support and foster long-term growth within the PiELaO community. While special sessions and issues offer valuable platforms for sharing research, we will go beyond these efforts by developing collaborative networks across different regions including Aisa, Europe and USA, developing benchmarks for some specific application domains and offering open resources of PiELaO in the implementations. More importantly, we will strengthen the foundation of the PiELaO community by joining forces with key stakeholders and leveraging diverse expertise over the regions. We will exploit multiple communication channels (e.g., newsletters, social media, webinars, conferences) to reach different segments of the PiELaO community and partnerships with other organizations to extend the PiELaO impact.
